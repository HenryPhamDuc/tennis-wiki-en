# Forehand Superiority

The documented physical and psychological advantage of the forehand stroke over the backhand, observed at both professional and recreational levels.

Forehand superiority is the biomechanical rationale underlying [Ambitennis](ambitennis.md) — if the forehand is the stronger weapon, the logical extension is to deploy it from both sides.

---

## The Physical Case

The forehand is typically stronger than the backhand for most players across skill levels. The mechanics of the forehand allow for:

- Greater kinetic chain involvement from the dominant arm and shoulder
- More comfortable contact-point range
- Superior reach on wide balls
- More natural power generation and timing

When a ball arrives on the forehand side, the typical player's mindset shifts toward offense. When the same ball arrives on the backhand side, offensive expectations lower. This psychological asymmetry reflects a real mechanical difference.

## Evidence from Professional Play

The professional tour's historical adaptations reflect forehand dominance:

- The **inside forehand** (running around the backhand to hit a forehand) became one of the most devastating baseline weapons in the game, developed systematically by Nick Bollettieri with Jim Courier in the late 1980s
- Courier's world number one ranking was built partly on a game plan that maximized forehand use and minimized backhand exposure
- The **inside-out forehand** became a standard professional weapon rather than a specialist shot

The fact that top players routinely run around their backhand — accepting worse court position in exchange for hitting a forehand — is direct evidence that the forehand is the preferred and stronger shot.

## Implications for Ambitennis

If the forehand is stronger, and if the [Overlapping Dual Forehand](overlapping-dual-forehand.md) makes it mechanically feasible to hit a forehand from the backhand side, then the backhand becomes an unnecessary compromise. [Ambitennis](ambitennis.md) takes forehand superiority to its logical conclusion.
