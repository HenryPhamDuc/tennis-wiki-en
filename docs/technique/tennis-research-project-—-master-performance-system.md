# Tennis Research Project — Master Performance System

A 12-chapter synthesis covering biomechanics, physiology, the inner game, and tactical execution. The central thread running through every chapter is the same: **power flows from the ground through the kinetic chain to the racket — and any break in that chain forces the arm to compensate, causing both performance failure and injury.**

---

## The Unifying Principle

The arm is not the engine. It is the whip.

The engine is the interaction between the feet and the court. Every major fault described in this system — [Arming](arming.md), the Noodle Core, the Hips-First Failure, the Late Lunge — is a variation of the same break: energy that was supposed to travel from the ground through the chain is instead generated by the arm alone, producing weak shots and overuse injury.

Mastery is the progressive elimination of these breaks until the Sequential Transfer fires automatically — what the Inner Game framework calls the shift from *making it happen* to *letting it happen*.
