# Pre-Performance Imagery

Pre-Performance Imagery (also called Psycho-Kinesiology or Mental Imagery Loading) is the practice of multi-sensory visualization of a specific shot sequence — trajectory, spin, contact, outcome — before the point begins, with the neurological purpose of uploading that instruction set directly to the [Basal Ganglia](../technique/basal-ganglia.md). Once the point starts, conscious thought is abandoned, and the body executes the pre-loaded biomechanical sequence automatically.

This technique completes the [C-to-I Transition](../technique/c-to-i-transition.md) *before* execution begins, rather than relying on it to happen correctly under time pressure.

---

## How It Works

By vividly visualizing the specific trajectory and spin of the intended shot (e.g., picturing the Magnus effect arc of a heavy topspin ball dipping into the corner), the player activates the same neural circuits that will fire during physical execution. The motor cortex, basal ganglia, and cerebellum process the imagery as a dry-run of the engram.

This serves two neurological functions:

1. **Engram priming**: the [Motor Engram](../technique/motor-engram.md) for the intended shot is brought into a heightened readiness state — "pre-loaded" — so it fires faster and with higher fidelity when triggered
2. **PFC displacement**: by fully occupying the analytical mind with a specific vivid sequence, the technique progressively shifts cognitive load from the [Prefrontal Cortex](../tactics/prefrontal-cortex.md) to the implicit system. By the time the point starts, there is no cognitive space for Self 1 to interfere

---

## Application

The technique is applied in the inter-point window (the 20-second reset period between points). The player does not think about the *mechanics* of execution — that is the failure mode that re-engages the PFC. They think about the *outcome* and *trajectory*: the ball landing on a specific spot, the particular visual of the spin pattern, the sound of the contact.

During the point itself, conscious thought is abandoned. The body simply executes the pre-loaded sequence. This maintains [Mushin](mushin.md) even under break-point pressure.

---

## Relationship to Choking Prevention

Pre-Performance Imagery is particularly powerful as a choking prevention tool because it addresses the choking mechanism upstream. Rather than trying to manage [Neural Reversion](neural-reversion.md) after it has occurred, it pre-empts it by completing the C-to-I Transition before the pressure of the moment can trigger the [Amygdala Hijack](../technique/amygdala-hijack.md).
