# Flow, Mushin, and Satori

The manuals treat **Flow**, **Mushin**, and **Satori** as different names for the same high-performance state: action without noisy self-interruption. The player is still aware, but the action runs with very little conscious clutter.

## Core idea

- Too much self-talk breaks timing.
- Clarity comes from less interference, not more effort.
- The best decisions feel automatic because they are already trained.

## Why it matters

This state protects technique under pressure. It also links psychology to neuro-motor performance, which is why the manuals connect it to myelination and automated logic.
