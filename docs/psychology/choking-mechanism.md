# Choking Mechanism

The Choking Mechanism is the full neurological sequence that converts competitive pressure into physical execution failure. It is not a mental weakness or a character flaw — it is the brain's survival architecture (designed for physical threat response) misfiring in a performance context.

Understanding the choking mechanism as a neurological sequence — rather than a psychological one — is the prerequisite for effectively dismantling it.

---

## The Full Sequence

1. **Pressure trigger**: high-stakes moment (break point, match point, tight third set)
2. **[Amygdala Hijack](../technique/amygdala-hijack.md)**: the amygdala detects threat and activates the sympathetic nervous system — fight-or-flight
3. **Cortisol flood**: the brain loses trust in its automated, implicit systems
4. **[Neural Reversion](neural-reversion.md)**: control forcibly reassigned from [Basal Ganglia](../technique/basal-ganglia.md) (implicit) to [Prefrontal Cortex](../tactics/prefrontal-cortex.md) (explicit)
5. **Explicit steering attempt**: the player consciously tries to manage a stroke that executes in 150ms
6. **Timing failure**: the PFC is ~200ms too slow; the execution window passes before the conscious correction arrives
7. **[Petit Bras](../technique/petit-bras.md)**: racket decelerates, grip tightens, kinetic chain shortens — the physical manifestation of the entire sequence

---

## What Makes It So Reliable

The choking mechanism is reliable because each step activates the next automatically. The amygdala cannot be voluntarily suppressed once triggered. The sympathetic nervous system fires below conscious awareness. Neural Reversion is not a choice — the brain withdraws trust from the implicit system as a survival response.

The only effective interventions are *upstream* — preventing the amygdala activation from propagating, or establishing such deeply myelinated implicit control that the implicit system can maintain execution even under partial sympathetic interference.

## Intervention Points

| Point in Sequence | Intervention |
|---|---|
| Before the point | [Thalamic Automaticity](../technique/thalamic-automaticity.md) (ritual to suppress PFC pre-emptively) |
| Before the point | [Pre-Performance Imagery](pre-performance-imagery.md) (complete C-to-I transition before execution) |
| During arousal buildup | Parasympathetic breathing (vagal activation, heart rate reduction) |
| First physical warning signs | Recognize death grip / rapid eye movement → run ritual immediately |
| Training environment | [Transient Hypofrontality](../technique/transient-hypofrontality.md) drills — building familiarity with implicit execution under overload |
