# Aging Athlete Protocol

The Aging Athlete Protocol is a specialized approach for players 50 and older, focusing on injury prevention, tendon elasticity management, and movement economy to prolong high-level play.

It acknowledges that older players must win through efficiency rather than raw explosiveness.

---

## Core Mechanism / How It Works
As players age, reaction times slow, tendon stiffness increases, and recovery windows lengthen. The protocol dictates a shift away from heavy rotational overload and brute force. Instead, it emphasizes:
- **Economical Footwork:** Using the [Crossover Step](../biomechanics/crossover-step.md) to reduce total steps.
- **Elastic Power:** Relying heavily on [Ground Reaction Forces (GRF)](../technique/ground-reaction-forces-(grf).md) and [Lag Mechanics](../technique/lag-mechanics.md) to generate pace without stressing the shoulder.
- **Recovery:** Prioritizing sleep, nutrition, and targeted mobility work over high-volume grinding.

## Failure Modes / Common Errors / When It Breaks
| Failure Mode | Cause | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| Playing Like a 20-Year-Old | Attempting massive, violent swings and extreme court coverage | Chronic injuries (tennis elbow, torn meniscus, lower back pain) |
| Ignoring Recovery | Playing 5 days a week without rest or mobility work | Systemic fatigue and rapid decline in match performance |

## Training / Application / Implementation
Older players should replace "junk miles" (endless rallying) with highly focused, short-duration deliberate practice. Warm-ups must be longer and focus on dynamic mobility, and match play should rely heavily on [Percentage Tennis](../technique/percentage-tennis.md) and [Winning Patterns](../tactics/winning-patterns.md) to minimize physical exertion.
